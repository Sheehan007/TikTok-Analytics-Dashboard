# Product Insights and Recommendations

## Executive Summary

The generated dataset simulates a scaled ads product analytics workflow across North America, Europe, Middle East and Africa, and Asia Pacific. It is designed to surface the same style of questions a product analytics team would bring into weekly business reviews: market health, funnel quality, advertiser retention, and product efficiency.

## Key Findings

- Conversion-led campaigns produce the strongest ROAS, but performance varies by advertiser maturity. Enterprise and Agency accounts show better setup quality and higher conversion rates.
- SMB advertisers create meaningful top-of-funnel volume, but lower CVR and D30 retention suggest campaign setup and optimization friction.
- High-growth markets with strong ROAS should be prioritized for scaled product marketing playbooks.
- Low-conversion country and segment combinations should receive workflow improvements, onboarding nudges, and clearer campaign objective recommendations.

## Product Recommendations

1. Improve low-conversion advertiser segments
   - Add in-product diagnostics for campaigns with high clicks but low conversions.
   - Recommend objective changes when CTR is healthy but CVR trails benchmark.

2. Optimize campaign setup workflows
   - Surface pixel, catalog, and creative readiness checks before launch.
   - Create SMB-specific setup templates for conversion and lead generation campaigns.

3. Prioritize high-ROAS markets
   - Increase product marketing investment in markets with ROAS above benchmark and positive revenue growth.
   - Package market-level success patterns into repeatable campaign setup guides.

4. Monitor retention by segment
   - Tie D30 advertiser retention to campaign activation quality.
   - Create lifecycle interventions for segments with strong spend but weak repeat usage.

5. Use weekly business review guardrails
   - Track ROAS, CPA, CVR, and revenue growth by market and objective.
   - Flag segments where spend grows faster than attributed revenue.
